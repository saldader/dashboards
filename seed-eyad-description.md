## Overview — What you're building

**A 6–7 page graphically strong PDF prospectus** that Izzy emails to a prospect **after their first discovery call.**

**Why it exists — the central problem this solves**

When a prospect is excited on the call, they go home and re-pitch RunRec to *their* investors, family, business partners. **Without a leave-behind, the message gets watered down.** Brian's exact example: Andre in Atlanta — passionate on the call, but when he tells his investors, it sounds like *"I talked to these guys Izzy and Brian, we'd open a gym, there's some software, I think it's on my phone..."*

The prospectus does the selling when we're **not in the room.** The prospect's investor reads it cold and gets it. Secondary use: trade-show leave-behind if/when we attend.

## What this is NOT

This is not the Instagram infographics (separate project — Sal handling with Mono / PGC). This is not a franchisee-community marketing asset Brian briefly mentioned for a future build. **Just the post-discovery-call PDF prospect-facing leave-behind.**

## Non-negotiables

Hard rules. Do not deviate without sign-off from Sal.

### Do not

- **No revenue figures or income claims.** Cannot say "you can earn X." Legally restricted until RunRec has an Item 19 in its FDD.
- **No invented pages.** Don't add "Our Team" / "Roadmap" / "FAQ" unless explicitly asked.
- **Don't exceed Brian's structure.** 6–7 pages total: title + 5–6 salient points + CTA. Not more.
- **Don't fill gaps with generic copy.** If a section is unclear, ping Sal — Brian's specifics carry weight.

### Always

- **Cost-to-establish numbers go IN** — with disclaimers. ($300–370K, $80K minimum capital, etc.) See *"Brian's legal stance"* below.
- **Add disclaimer on every financial number** — "Costs vary by community. See FDD for details."
- Put the **version number on the cover page** (small, footer-style).
- **Few words per page.** Graphically very simple, very clean. Easy to read.

## Brian's legal stance — read carefully

Brian called this out directly. Two camps disagree on what can go in a prospectus:

- **Eleanor (RunRec lawyer):** would say no — "one source, FDD only. Nope."
- **Franchise development professionals:** "It's a bit extreme. We can take the same information from the FDD and put it in a prospectus."

**Brian's call (and what we're shipping):** cost-to-establish numbers go IN with disclaimers. Revenue / income claims stay OUT.

> "There is some risk to it, but there's lots of reward and the risk is pretty small because we're not really misrepresenting."

## Page-by-page

Brian's structure: **Title page + 5–6 salient points + CTA = 6–7 pages total.** Each section below carries Brian's exact direction.

### Page 1 — Title Page

Cover · brand · version

**Content**

- **Title:** RunRec Franchisee Prospectus *(or Franchisee Information)*
- Strong cover image — basketball, court, RunRec branding
- Version number bottom corner: **v1.0**
- Date

### Page 2 — What is RunRec / Why RunRec

The setup — define the model in one page.

**Purpose.** The orientation page. A reader who's never spoken to us understands what RunRec is and why it exists in 30 seconds.

**Content to include**

- One-line definition: *"RunRec is a 24/7/365 staffless basketball facility."*
- 3–4 bullets on what makes us different
- Visual: facility photo or hero shot of court

> "What is RunRec? Why RunRec? That's the whole — graphically very simple, very clean, not a lot of words."

### Page 3 — The Basketball Industry

Show the size of the wave.

**Purpose.** Basketball coaches don't know the market data. We do. Plant the "this is huge" feeling.

**Stats to include (Brian's exact callouts)**

- **$7 billion+** total basketball market
- **$14.3 billion** equipment & apparel industry
- Equipment / apparel growing at **5.3%**
- **40+ million** basketball players in North America *(source: Sal's LinkedIn post — pull citation)*
- Headline: *"Be part of a $7 billion growing industry."*

**Visual treatment.** Tight stats with clean charts/graphs. No clutter.

> "I can even envision pulling some tight stats and seeing some graphs and some charts on that page."

### Page 4 — 24/7/365 Staffless Model

Why our business model is incredibly powerful.

**The page's whole thesis.** This isn't a feature list — the entire page argues *"why our business model is incredibly powerful."* Brian's framing: contrast our model against the pain every other business owner lives with.

> "Why our business model makes so much sense. Why our business model is incredibly powerful... You know the biggest challenge in owning a business is trying to manage staffing... Many owners of any business have the biggest challenge of being tied to the operation of their business and so their family vacations are kind of etc. — Not RunRec."

**Supporting bullets**

- 24 hours / 7 days / 365 days
- Fully staffless — automated entry, booking, payments
- Proprietary software runs the gym

**Visual ideas (Brian's direct suggestions)**

> "Picture of someone sitting on their sofa with a cup of coffee, phone in hand, RunRec app open. Caption: 'Monitor your gym from anywhere.'"

> "Or: someone grabbing the door of the facility with a clock emblem above. Caption: 'Proprietary software lets your customers come in 24/7.'"

### Page 5 — Coach Testimonials

"Why Our Coaches Love Us" — social proof from real, passionate coaches.

**Brian's vision (verbatim)**

> "You can picture an entire page where 'our coaches love us' with a picture of a coach standing there with a whistle around their neck and a little testimonial beside them."

**Layout**

- Photo of a real coach in a basketball setting (whistle, on-court, real environment — **not stock**)
- Short pull quote (1–2 sentences)
- Coach name + city

**Source.** Sal will provide testimonials and photos. If unpolished by v1.0, mock up with placeholder; swap in v1.1.

**Note on "Andre."** If Sal mentions "Andre" — that's a coach in Atlanta Brian referenced in the meeting as an example of the *watered-down-pitch problem* the prospectus solves. Brian was **not** giving casting direction for this page's photo. Don't try to recreate "sweating in his car" energy unless Sal explicitly tells you to.

### Page 6 — Why Ownership is Good for You

Lifestyle + income control — emotional close.

**Purpose.** Speak to **the person**, not the business.

**Brian's framing (verbatim phrases to use)**

- *"You control your income level."*
- *"Many of our owners are looking to open 3 to 10 RunRecs."*
- *"I love to coach my kids' basketball and soccer."*
- *"I love the freedom to go watch a college basketball game."*
- *"I like to cut my lawn in the middle of the afternoon."*
- *"Did you know that with RunRec, you determine how much income you make?"*

**Visual treatment.** Lifestyle imagery — coaching kids, family, freedom. Make them **feel** it.

### Page 7 — Investment + Capital Access

Demystify the money. Show this is reachable.

**Investment estimate**

- **$300,000 – $370,000** to set up
- Disclaimer: *"Costs vary by community. See FDD for full details."*

**How franchise partners access this capital (Brian's exact list)**

- **Personal savings**
- **401(k) access** — up to **$50,000** or **50% of balance**, whichever is less *(small disclaimer footnote)*
- **Small business loan** (SBA loan)
- **Family investment**
- **Home equity / line of credit** — example: house worth $400K, $300K paid off → access ~$200K HELOC

**RunRec minimum requirement**

> "RunRec requires a minimum of $80,000 in available capital to invest in your project. Your bank may require more."

**Visual treatment.** Clean buckets / icons for each capital path. Bowling-lane prospectus layout is the reference.

### Page 8 — Call to Action

Last page — clear next step.

**Content**

- One strong CTA — *"Book your franchise discovery call"* or *"Take the next step"*
- Email + phone + booking link
- RunRec logo
- Version number footer: **v1.0**
